360° Automotive Speaker Should-Cost Engine — User Guide

This tool models the complete factory-gate cost of an automotive speaker system manufactured in China (Ex-Works). It covers direct materials, direct labour, machine overhead, tooling amortisation, G&A, warranty, and system integration costs — calibrated to June 2026 SHFE/SMM commodity prices. Use it to benchmark supplier quotes, build business cases, and explore cost-reduction trade-offs.

Step-by-Step Instructions
1

Select Manufacturing Country

Choose where the system will be manufactured. China (default) applies EXW China factory-gate economics — Guangdong labour rates (¥2,500/month minimum wage basis), domestic SHFE/SMM commodity pricing, and Chinese machine hour costs at 11% of Western European reference rates.

💡 China Coastal (Shanghai/Guangzhou) carries a 12% machine multiplier and ¥6.5/hr effective labour vs China Inland's 9% and ¥4.5/hr. Choose based on your actual supplier location.
2

Set Annual Production Volume

Volume directly affects tooling amortisation per unit. A Full-Range basket tool costing $50,000 amortises to $0.23/unit at 213,000 annual pieces, but $5.00/unit at 10,000. Set volume to your platform programme's target annual build rate.

💡 Presets (50k, 100k, 213k, 500k, 1M) cover typical automotive tiers. Premium platforms typically run 100k–250k/year. Volume above 500k triggers shared-die economics not currently modelled.
3

Select System Tier

Choose the OEM system architecture: Standard (6 spk passive), Enhanced (8 spk + amp + DSP), Premium (13 spk flagship), Immersive 3D (20 spk spatial audio), or Immersive 4D (24 spk + haptic seat actuators). Each pre-configures the driver count, placement, amplifier, DSP, and wiring harness.

⚠ The system architecture determines which driver types are included. You can customise individual driver materials but not add/remove driver positions in this version.
4

Configure Magnet Type

The magnet is typically the single largest cost item (30–45% of BOM). Options: NdFeB Sintered ($62/kg — highest performance, largest cost), Ferrite ($1.30/kg — lowest cost, heavier, lower flux), SmCo ($95/kg — ultra high-temp, aerospace), Bonded NdFeB ($30/kg — complex shapes), Recycled NdFeB ($55/kg — sustainability use case).

💡 NdFeB is standard for all premium automotive. Ferrite is acceptable only for rear-fill speakers where packaging weight and cost matter more than performance.
5

Select Basket / Frame Material

Die-cast aluminium ($3.50/kg) is the premium choice — light, rigid, excellent heat dissipation from voice coil via the basket structure. Stamped steel ($0.62/kg) is cost-optimised for non-critical positions. Injection PP ($1.45/kg) suits door-mounted speakers with vibration isolation requirements.

💡 For flagship (Premium / Immersive) systems, die-cast Al is expected by most OEMs. Using stamped steel in a premium quotation will be challenged in design review.
6

Select Voice Coil Wire Material

Wire material sets resistance, weight, and thermal budget. OFC Copper ($16/kg) — lowest DCR, best power handling, premium. CCA (Copper-Clad Aluminium) ($8/kg) — 10% Cu shell on 90% Al core; lighter and cheaper at marginal conductivity penalty. CCAW ($7/kg) — self-bonding CCA used in fine-gauge coils. Flat Copper ($17/kg) — edge-wound coils for maximum fill factor in high-power subwoofers.

7

Choose Cone Material

Cone material determines frequency response character, mass, and stiffness. Paper ($0.75/kg) — best internal damping, lightest, OEM standard. PP ($1.38/kg) — moisture-resistant, consistent, suitable for door locations. Composite CF/GF ($3.20/kg) — high stiffness for extended HF. Carbon Fibre ($34/kg) — ultra-premium, used in flagship tweets. Kevlar ($22/kg) — unique damping character, Dynaudio/Focal signature.

8

Choose Surround Material

The surround sets Xmax, Qms, and long-term reliability. Rubber (NBR) ($1.85/kg) — durable, UV-stable, used in door woofers. PU Foam ($0.90/kg) — low mass, good Qts control; ageing is the key risk in high-humidity environments. Cloth ($2.50/kg) — used in tweeters and some mid-ranges. Santoprene ($4.00/kg) — thermoplastic elastomer, consistent moulding, excellent -40°C performance.

9

Select Voice Coil Former

The former sets thermal management and HF extension. Aluminium former ($3.70/kg) — conducts heat from coil to gap; preferred for power-handling. Kapton (Polyimide) ($9.50/kg) — ultra-thin wall, light, extends HF. Nomex ($18/kg) — Aramid paper, high-temp subwoofer use. Paper former ($0.90/kg) — lowest mass, suitable for low-power applications.

10

Add Technology Features

Optional system-level features add cost as fixed amounts to the system total. Each feature has been costed at China EXW: ANC (+$14) adds microphone array and DSP algorithm licence cost. 4D Haptics (+$48) adds seat actuator controllers. Neural DSP (+$45) adds AI-audio processor cost. Multiple features can be combined.

💡 Technology features are modelled as add-on system costs. They do not currently affect individual speaker-level BOM — future versions will integrate microphone cost into the per-speaker BOM.
11

Set Commercial Parameters

Target Margin % — applied to total landed cost. Set to 0% for pure EXW should-cost (no margin). Set to 15–25% for Tier-1 supplier sell price estimate. Freight Mode — Domestic (within China, $0.28/unit base), Regional (Asia, $0.80), International ($2.50). Import Duty — enter duty rate for destination country. UK: 3.5–6.5% on speakers; EU: 4.2–6.5%. Set 0% for EXW baseline.

⚠ Setting margin > 0% converts EXW cost to Tier-1 sell price. Keep at 0% when benchmarking pure factory-gate should-cost against supplier quotes.
12

Click Calculate

The engine runs all 14 manufacturing process steps across each driver type, scales by size, applies country multipliers, amortises tooling, adds system-level costs, then sums to a total. Calculation is instantaneous (all in-browser, no server call). Results populate across 9 tabs simultaneously.

💡 You can use Quick Preset buttons (Standard / Enhanced / Premium) to load pre-configured scenarios instantly, then tweak individual parameters.
13

Reading the System Overview Tab

The overview shows: top 5 metric KPIs (EXW total, speaker count, DM%, CO₂, margin), a per-driver breakdown table (DM, Labour, Mach, Unit works, Total), a cost waterfall bar chart, and system-level add-ons (amp, DSP, wiring, connectors). The waterfall shows how each cost layer builds to the total.

14

Analysing the Full BOM

The BOM tab shows every material line item per driver type: material key, mass in grams, cost per driver, cost per system (qty × unit). RoHS / REACH / VOC compliance flags are shown per material. Useful for: confirming commodity exposure, identifying highest-cost materials, flagging compliance risks early.

15

Benchmarking Tab

Compares your system's EXW China cost against 10 reference systems (Bose, Harman Kardon, B&O, Burmester, etc.). Reference est_cost values are EXW China factory-gate estimates derived from public teardown data and analyst sources — calibrated Jun 2026. The delta column shows your cost advantage or premium vs each benchmark.

16

Scenario Compare

Save current result as Scenario A, reconfigure inputs, run again, then save as Scenario B. The Compare tab produces a side-by-side delta table across all cost lines (DM, Labour, Machine, FOH, G&A, Tooling, Warranty, Amp, DSP, Wiring) plus a visual bar chart. Ideal for presenting NdFeB-vs-Ferrite trade-offs or Premium-vs-Enhanced system business cases.

17

AI Insights Tab

Triggers a call to the Claude AI model. The AI receives your full cost result and generates: executive summary, top 3 cost drivers, material substitution opportunities, process efficiency recommendations, and market context from current automotive audio trends. Requires an Anthropic API key (configured via the API Key button in the header).

💡 AI insights are regenerated each time you run a new calculation with different inputs. The stream is abortable — clicking Calculate again cancels any in-progress generation.
18

Export to PowerPoint

The Export PPT button (header) generates a boardroom-quality slide deck: cover slide, executive summary KPIs, per-driver BOM table, cost waterfall, benchmarking table, sustainability score, and process routing. Uses PptxGenJS — all in-browser, no server dependency. Download is automatic on click.

Master Cost Equation

How Total Cost is Computed

The engine computes cost bottom-up for each driver type, then sums with system-level additions and commercial adjustments:

DM = Σ (material_mass_kg × material_rate_USD/kg) [per driver] DLab = Σ (step.baseMin × step.labFrac × labHr/60) [per step] DMach = Σ (step.baseMin × (1−step.labFrac) × step.machRate × machM / 60) prime = DM + DLab + DMach FOH (12%) = prime × 0.12 mfgCost = prime + FOH G&A (5%) = mfgCost × 0.05 [China rate; Western = 7%] toolAmort = PROF.tool_total / annualVolume warranty = mfgCost × 0.015 inbound = DM × 0.02 [China domestic sourcing] works = mfgCost + G&A + toolAmort + warranty + inbound sysItems = SPK_SYS costs (housing + grille + bracket + connector) totalPerUnit = works + sysItems systemTotal = Σ(totalPerUnit × qty) + amp + DSP + wiring + connectors + features + outboundFreight + importDuty + margin

All computation is in USD. Final display converts to GBP at rate £1 = $1.27 (FX_GBP = 1/1.27).

Material Rate Derivation (MRATE)

How Commodity Prices Are Converted to Factory-Gate Material Rates

MRATE values represent the factory-gate finished material cost at a Chinese speaker manufacturer, not raw commodity spot prices. Each rate is derived as:

  • Raw spot price — from SHFE (copper, aluminium, steel), SMM/Asian Metal (rare earths), or domestic petrochemical indices (PP/PA66)
  • Processing premium — wire drawing, enamelling, forming, alloying, injection, die-casting — typically 10–25% above ingot
  • Exchange rate — ¥7.25/USD applied to all CNY prices (Jun 2026 mid-rate)
  • Supplier margin — included in finished material price from distributor to speaker factory

14-Step Manufacturing Process Model (STEPS)

How Process Costs Are Computed

Each of 14 process steps is defined by a machine rate (USD/hr, Western European reference), a base cycle time (minutes), and a labour fraction (what percentage of the step time is direct labour vs machine-paced). The country's machM multiplier scales the machine rate to local capital cost basis.

labCost per step = baseMin × labFrac × (country.labHr / 60) machCost per step = baseMin × (1 − labFrac) × (step.machRate × country.machM / 60) scrapCost per step = (labCost + machCost) × step.scrap_rate

A size scale factor Math.pow(sizeMM / refMM, 1.3) scales all process costs proportionally to driver diameter. A 300mm subwoofer takes ~3.5× the process time of a 165mm full-range.

Country Labour & Machine Rate Multipliers

Why China machM = 0.11 (Not 0.78)

A common error in speaker cost modelling is applying a machine rate that reflects the same Western capital cost basis. In reality, Chinese speaker factories achieve machine hour costs of 10–12% of Western European reference rates due to:

  • Lower capex amortisation — plant investment amortised at Chinese land/construction cost (¥3,000–5,000/m²) vs Western (€15,000–25,000/m²)
  • Lower energy cost — industrial electricity ¥0.65–0.85/kWh China vs €0.18–0.28/kWh Germany
  • Lower maintenance labour — maintenance technicians at ¥8,000–12,000/month vs €3,500–5,500/month Western Europe
  • Equipment procurement advantage — Chinese-made tooling/presses at 30–50% of European equivalent cost
⚠ Using machM = 0.78 (as many tools default to) inflates machine cost by 7× vs reality, causing total system cost to be estimated 2.5–3.5× too high for China manufacturing.

Overhead Structure & Cost Additions

Factory Overhead (FOH) — 12% of Prime Cost

Applied to prime = DM + DLab + DMach. Covers: facility depreciation, indirect labour (supervisors, quality engineers, maintenance), utilities, production consumables (cutting oils, cleaning agents, packaging materials for WIP), and quality system costs (ISO/IATF 16949 audit overhead). The 12% rate is typical for Chinese Tier-1/2 automotive speaker factories at volume.

G&A (General & Administrative) — 5% of mfgCost (China)

Covers head office, finance, HR, legal, IT, and sales overhead allocated to the manufacturing cost. Chinese speaker manufacturers operating at Tier-2 level typically run 4–6% G&A on manufactured goods. Western equivalents run 6–9%. The tool uses 5% for all China variants and 7% for Western countries.

Tooling Amortisation

Each driver type carries a set of dedicated hard tooling (basket die, VC winding fixture, cone mould, spider press, assembly jig, NRE/DFM engineering). Tooling cost is divided by annual volume to give a $/unit charge:

toolAmort = (basket + vc + cone + spider + fixture + nre) / annualVolume

Example — Full-Range at 213,000/year: $50,000+$9,000+$15,000+$9,000+$5,000+$28,000 = $116,000 ÷ 213,000 = $0.545/unit. At 10,000/year the same tooling costs $11.60/unit — a 21× difference that makes low-volume programmes uneconomical without amortisation negotiation.

Warranty — 1.5% of mfgCost

Automotive Tier-1/2 standard warranty provision. Covers: field returns, rework cost, replacement logistics, and goodwill. 1.5% is industry-standard for passive speaker assemblies with no electronics. Active systems (with DSP/amp) carry higher warranty provisions (2.5–4%) which are embedded in the purchased component cost.

Inbound Logistics — 2% of DM (China)

Cost of moving raw materials from Chinese domestic suppliers to the speaker factory. China domestic freight is very efficient: magnets from Baotou/Ganzhou, copper wire from Guangdong wire mills, PP from Daqing/Guangzhou petrochemical plants. 2% of DM is appropriate for domestic China sourcing. Western countries use 3% (longer supply chains, higher freight rates).

System-Level Cost Additions

After per-driver costs are summed, the following system-level costs are added:

  • Amplifier — direct China EXW price (no machM multiplier). A 7-channel Class D automotive amp with integrated protection = $38 at volume from Chinese OEM amp manufacturers (e.g. Hypex-equivalent domestic brands).
  • DSP — similarly direct EXW. 12-channel automotive DSP with EQ/crossover processing = $22 from domestic suppliers. Premium neural-DSP with AI audio = add $45 separately via Technology Features.
  • Wiring harness — total harness (speaker cables + main harness) at $10 for 20m + $0.22/m cable × 32m = $17.04 for Premium system.
  • Connectors — $0.22/connector China EXW for automotive-grade (USCAR-2 / ISO 20696 equivalent) multi-pin speaker connectors at volume.
  • Housing / Bracket / Grille per driver — SPK_SYS lookup scaled by driver size. Full-Range: housing $1.65, grille $0.55, bracket $0.65, connector $0.28 = $3.13 total per driver position.

🧲

Sintered NdFeB

N38H
BHmax 38 MGOe, Hcj 17 kOe. Standard premium automotive tweeter magnet. Good balance of flux density and high-temp coercivity.
Premium
N40H
BHmax 40 MGOe. Most common grade for mid-range and full-range automotive speakers. Specified for -40°C to +85°C operating range.
Premium
N42SH
BHmax 42 MGOe, Hcj 25 kOe (Super High coercivity). For door-mounted speakers exposed to high thermal cycling. Resists demagnetisation from stray fields.
Ultra
N48H
BHmax 48 MGOe. Flagship subwoofer and premium Tier-1 woofer applications. Highest flux in H-rated grades. Cost ~18% premium over N40H.
Ultra
N35
BHmax 35 MGOe, no H rating. Only for applications with Tmax <60°C. Avoided in automotive — max operating temp too low for door speakers.
Avoid Automotive

Price modelled at N40 grade. N42SH/N48H cost +12–18% premium. The H-suffix (Hcj ≥17 kOe) is mandatory for all automotive applications per most OEM material specifications.

⬛

Ferrite (Ceramic)

Y25 / Y30 / Y35 grades
Y30
BHmax 7.2 MGOe, Br 3,900 G. Most common automotive ferrite grade. Strontium ferrite sintered. Used in budget rear-fill and OEM passive systems.
Standard
Y35
BHmax 8.6 MGOe, Br 4,200 G. Enhanced ferrite. Approximately 19% more flux than Y30 at 8% cost premium. Used in cost-optimised mid-range drivers.
Standard
Y25
BHmax 6.0 MGOe. Entry-grade. Heavy (density 4.9 g/cm³) — significantly heavier magnet assembly than equivalent NdFeB. Restricted to weight-insensitive applications.
Budget

Ferrite magnets are 3–7× heavier than NdFeB for equivalent flux. This adds chassis weight and limits use in pillar/headrest tweeters. Temperature performance is actually better than NdFeB (improves with temperature).

💠

SmCo (Samarium Cobalt)

SmCo-18 / SmCo-26 / SmCo-28
SmCo-26
BHmax 26 MGOe, Tmax 350°C. Primary automotive grade. Used where Tmax >150°C or extreme demagnetisation resistance is required (engine bay, very high power density).
Ultra
SmCo-28
BHmax 28 MGOe. Highest commercially available SmCo grade. Extremely rare in speaker applications — primarily aerospace/defence. Cost 3× SmCo-26.
Speciality
SmCo-18
Lower energy density. The original SmCo grade. Almost entirely superseded by NdFeB for cost performance. Retained in some heritage designs.
Legacy

SmCo is used in <1% of automotive speaker programmes. Its primary value is Tmax >150°C. For normal automotive speaker positions (door, pillar, headrest), NdFeB with H/SH rating is always preferred.

♻

Bonded & Recycled NdFeB

Alternative magnet forms
Bonded NdFeB
NdFeB powder in epoxy binder. Complex shapes achievable (ring, arc, multi-pole). BHmax 6–12 MGOe — well below sintered. Used in headrest/satellite micro-speakers. Modelled at $30/kg.
Niche
Recycled NdFeB
Re-sintered from post-consumer or manufacturing scrap. BHmax typically 85–92% of virgin equivalent. SMM Jun 2026: ¥400/kg ($55/kg). Primary driver is OEM sustainability mandates. Modelled identically to virgin NdFeB in performance.
Sustainable

Recycled NdFeB receives CO₂ credit of ~18 kg CO₂e/kg vs ~45 kg CO₂e/kg for virgin sintered. This is reflected in the Sustainability tab CO₂ calculation.

Ti dome tweeters. Very stiff, low mass. Used in AMG/Mercedes, Bowers & Wilkins automotive. Hard dome — excellent off-axis if well-damped. Modelled $24/kg (Ti sheet).

Copper Wire Variants

OFC, CCA, CCAW, Flat Cu
OFC (Cu enamelled)
Oxygen-Free Copper, enamelled. Lowest DCR per unit mass. Standard for all premium automotive voice coils. Conductivity 101% IACS. Temperature class 155°C (F-grade enamel).
Premium
CCA wire
Copper-Clad Aluminium: Al core, Cu outer shell. 10% lighter than pure Cu at 95% conductivity. Used in cost-optimised mid-range and rear-fill woofers. CCA: $8/kg vs $16/kg Cu.
Cost-Opt
CCAW
Copper-Clad Aluminium Wire — fine-gauge self-bonding variant. Used in 4-layer and 2-layer thin coils. Self-bonding insulation eliminates varnish step. Modelled at $7/kg.
Standard
Flat / Edgewise Cu
Rectangular-section copper strip, edge-wound into coil. Maximum fill factor (98% vs 75% round wire). Used in high-power subwoofers (500W+). Requires precision edgewise winding machine. $17/kg.
Ultra
Ag-Cu (Silver-Cu)
Silver-alloyed copper wire. Marginal conductivity improvement; primary value is marketing differentiation. Very rare in production automotive. Modelled at $32/kg.

🌀

Coil Former Materials

Al, Kapton, Nomex, Ti
Aluminium
Highly conductive — transfers heat from coil winding to magnetic gap air. Also provides eddy current damping that reduces HF peaks. Best for power handling. $3.70/kg.
Standard Premium
Kapton (Polyimide)
Ultra-thin (25–50μm) cylindrical film. Lowest mass of all formers — extends HF bandwidth by reducing moving mass. Tmax 300°C. Used in tweeters and lightweight mid-range. $9.50/kg.
Ultra
Nomex
Aramid paper (DuPont). Rigid, high-temp (Tmax 220°C), low mass. Subwoofer high-power application. Less thermally conductive than Al but excellent structural integrity at high excursion. $18/kg.
Premium
Paper former
Phenolic resin-treated kraft paper. Lowest cost, adequate stiffness for low-power applications. Risk of deformation at high moisture + temperature. $0.90/kg.
Budget
Titanium
For ultra-high-power subwoofers (1kW+). Titanium alloy tube. Extreme stiffness prevents gap collision. Very rare — only in competition audio and flagship automotive. $20/kg.
Speciality
Glass Fibre (GF)
Woven GF tube. Moderate stiffness, low thermal conductivity vs Al. Cost-effective upgrade from paper. Used in mid-tier full-range and some mid-range designs. $2.20/kg.

Frame & Basket Materials

Die-Cast Aluminium

ADC12 / A380 alloy
ADC12 (A383)
Most common automotive speaker basket alloy. Si 9–12%, good fluidity for complex die geometries. Excellent thermal conductivity (96 W/m·K) — draws heat from magnet/VC assembly. Surface treated: powder coat or anodise.
Standard Premium
A380
Higher strength (324 MPa UTS), better corrosion resistance vs ADC12. Used where basket must also serve as structural bracket in the door cavity. Common in Tier-1 premium programmes.
Premium

Die-cast Al modelled at $3.50/kg. The die casting process (step: Basket Mfg) carries the highest machine rate in the model at $52.5/hr W.Eur reference — reflecting expensive high-pressure die casting equipment.

🔩

Stamped Steel

SPCC / SECC grades
SPCC (Cold-rolled)
Standard cold-rolled steel. SHFE domestic ¥4,200/MT. Cost-optimised basket for budget and mid-tier applications. Phosphated + electro-coated for corrosion protection. Heavier than Al (7.87 vs 2.7 g/cm³).
Budget
SECC (Electro-galv.)
SPCC with zinc electroplate. Improved corrosion resistance for door speaker positions. Used where sealing is incomplete. Zinc coating adds ~$0.04/kg to SPCC base price.
Standard

Steel basket is a common Tier-2 specification reduction. Most OEM premium programmes specify die-cast Al as a minimum. Switching from Al to SECC can save ~$1.40/driver but will be challenged on thermal management grounds in design reviews.

Plastic frames
PP + 30% GF
Glass-fibre reinforced PP. Lightweight, cost-effective. Excellent vibration isolation from door panel. Injection tooling cost is similar to stamped steel die. Used in non-structural mounting positions. $1.45/kg base + tooling.
Standard
PA66 + 30% GF
Nylon 66 glass-fibre reinforced. Higher Tmax than PP (240°C vs 165°C). Used in dash-mounted speakers with solar load exposure. $2.40/kg. Moisture absorption can cause dimensional creep — design must allow.

Very high internal loss (damping). Used for targeted mode control in specific speaker designs. Gas-impermeable — sometimes used as acoustic seal in sealed cabinets. $2.20/kg.

Standard Spider Fabric
Cotton/polyester woven fabric, phenolic resin-impregnated and thermoformed. Sets Qms, mechanical compliance (Cms), and Xmax range. All drivers use this material class. $3.00/kg.
