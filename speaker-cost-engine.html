Negotiation Insights & Cost Levers

Key Cost Drivers

Neodymium rare-earth magnet prices swing ±20%+ with geopolitical supply constraints
Voice coil copper wire tracks LME copper spot (±10–15% annual variance)
Die-cast basket tooling ($85k–$130k) dominates amortisation at low volumes
Sub-assembly labour is the largest process step — 2–2.5 min/unit, 70% manual
DSP calibration/testing adds fixed cost regardless of volume (spec dependent)

Negotiation & Design Levers

Ferrite vs. Neodymium: saves 15–40% on magnet cost; use for bass/mid applications
Aluminium VC wire saves ~75% on wire cost vs. copper (minor SPL trade-off)
Injection-moulded PP basket vs. die-cast Al saves $0.10–0.25/unit + lower tooling
Volume ramp to 100k+/yr reduces tooling amortisation from $4+/unit to <$0.50
Dual-source magnet supply (CN + EU) hedges rare-earth volatility by 10–15%
