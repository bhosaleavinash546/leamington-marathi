Executive Summary

01  The headline

On its core mild-hybrid (MHEV) programme — the only drivetrain the L460 shares with the BMW X7, Audi Q8 and Mercedes GLS — the L460's embedded software costs materially more than the German rivals. The cause is specific, and the fix is largely architectural rather than commercial.

Five findings

1
The L460's software costs ~38% more than the BMW X7 / Audi Q8 on the equivalent MHEV programme.

£420M vs £305M / £313M. Per vehicle the gap is smaller (£700 vs £636 / £631) because the L460 amortises over higher volume.

2
The gap is a software-reuse gap, not a geography or volume gap.

Holding region, source and volume constant (normalised view), the L460 still sits ~£155M above the Germans — because BMW and Audi carry far more software forward between models ("Heavy"/platform reuse) while the L460 develops more afresh.

3
The L460's commercial setup is already efficient.

Its Tier-1 supplier sourcing (0.88× vs in-house) and UK base are lowering its cost — so the opportunity is engineering strategy, not procurement.

4
Lifting reuse to peer level would save ~£145M (−35%) and reach parity with the Germans — no offshoring required.

Raising baseline reuse "Medium → Heavy" and adopting shared-platform middleware brings the programme to ~£275M.

5
On electrified variants the L460 already benchmarks favourably.

Against the only true peer with PHEV/BEV (Porsche Cayenne), the L460 is cheaper on both total and per-vehicle — the reuse issue is concentrated in the high-volume MHEV programme.

Transparency

08  Assumptions & methodology

Every figure is live output from the CostVision should-cost engine (49-module catalogue, ISO 26262 / 21434 framework). Nothing is hand-entered. The assumptions below are stated in full so each number can be challenged.

Model class & accuracy
Bottom-up parametric should-cost; validated envelope of ±25–35% (back-test MAPE 24.9%, 5 of 7 published programmes within ±35%). Absolute numbers are estimates; the comparisons are consistent because every programme uses the same engine and rate library.
Propulsion availability
Verified July 2026 against manufacturer / industry sources. X7 (G07) is MHEV-only (iX7 BEV is next-gen 2027+); Q8 & GLS are MHEV-only (their e-tron / EQS siblings are separate models); Cayenne offers ICE/PHEV/BEV (Electric premiered Nov 2025); Range Rover Electric is JLR's first EV, launching.
"Apple-to-apple"
Two views: as-is (each car on its real region, volume, life, reuse) and normalised (common EU / in-house / 60k-yr basis, only software strategy varying). Comparisons are only drawn where both cars build the drivetrain.
Reuse factors
Applied to development effort: Fresh 1.00× · Light 0.82× · Medium 0.60× · Heavy 0.35× · Platform 0.14×. "Baseline reuse" is the programme default before per-module overrides.
Per-car assumptions (MHEV)
L460: UK · Tier-1 · 75k/yr · 8yr · Medium
X7: EU · in-house · 60k/yr · 8yr · Heavy
Q8: EU · in-house · 55k/yr · 9yr · Heavy + platform middleware
GLS: EU · in-house · 45k/yr · 9yr · Medium (MBUX signature)
Cayenne: EU · in-house · 50k/yr · 8yr · Medium + platform + Porsche performance
Signature software
Each car keeps its brand-DNA overrides (Meridian audio on L460; MBUX / voice on GLS; Porsche 4D chassis / Active Ride on Cayenne; UWB digital key on X7), held constant across its drivetrains.
What "saving" means
Reduction in total programme software should-cost (NRE + lifecycle) versus the L460 as-is baseline, from changing the stated lever only.
Currency & scope
All figures GBP. Embedded & connected vehicle software only; excludes manufacturing, hardware and non-software engineering.

Some cross-car comparisons hold volume/region constant to isolate a variable; where a per-vehicle figure is quoted it also reflects that car's production volume. This report supports target-setting and supplier negotiation; it is not an audited actual. Engine: CostVision sw-should-cost.ts, rate library v1.0, July 2026.
