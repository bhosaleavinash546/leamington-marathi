The method, in five moves

02How the number is built

No figure is guessed at the top. The programme is broken into modules, each is costed from first principles, results roll up, then the total is stress-tested for risk and sanity.

2

3

Roll up

4

Validate

Read this before you quote the number

11Method & assumptions

An envelope, not audited-to-the-pound. This is a ±25–35% parametric should-cost model. Its value is that it decomposes and sources every figure, and reports its own confidence band — not that it predicts the actual to the last pound.

What it is good for

  • Target-setting — a defensible number to negotiate a supplier quote against.
  • What-if analysis — move region, reuse or ASIL and see the £ impact instantly.
  • Make-vs-buy & offshore — the region and dev-source levers are explicit.

Where to be careful

  • Costs by domain, not by brand — captures the cost of a system, not every named ECU.
  • Inputs drive everything — a wrong reuse or ASIL assumption moves the total materially.
  • Benchmarks are estimates — the validation targets are analyst figures, not audited books.
